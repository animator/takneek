    BlackBox is an on-the-spot problem solving and programming competition restricted for the first year junta ONLY.

    Points: 20

    Team:
    Maximum of 2 members. No restrictions on the number of teams from a pool.

    Format
    Team Event
    Consists of 2 rounds

    Prelims - Written round consisting of Logical questions, mathematical puzzles and basic C programming questions. The top 30 teams will qualify for the final rounds. In case of a tie, at the 30th position either all teams would qualify for the second round or none. However teams qualifying for the final round would also have to clear the minimum cut-off of 25% of total score.

    Finals - The final round would be conducted in Computer Center where the teams would be required to code in an unknown language (not C, C++, Java or Python). This is an 2 hour event and the team with the maximum number of correct submissions in the minimum time would walk away as the winners.

    Rules -
    1. This is a Team Event. However, the minimum eligibility criterion for each 30 teams is that each team must have at least 30% of the correct answers
    2. In the second round the rules follow from the Kodefest that if the top 4 teams should have 20% correct submissions.
    3. If any team is found cheating then that team would be disqualified from the event.

    CodeSprint is an online competition along the same lines as Crypto.
    CodeSprint would consist of several levels, you have to answer the question or Hack the question to reach to the next level. Questions would be from Hacking, Programming and Mathematical Logic.

    Points: 20

    Team
    CodeSprint is a POOL event.
    You can register Individually.

    Rules and Regulations
    1. This is a Pool event.
    2. Points would be decided on the basis of top individual of each pool and his overall ranking.
    3. Points of each individual will be the cumulative score of all questions answered by him till the end of the competition.
    4. As this is a POOL event you can share your answer with your POOL members.
    5. Any sort of attempt to contact moderators personally for answers will lead to disqualification of the participant.
    6. The discretion and judgment of the moderators will be final.
    7. You can skip any level and go to the next level.

    Are you one of those who geeks who dream of building their own processor? Or do you want to do something that sets you apart from the rest of the electro maniacs? If yes, then, we have something great for you. Yes! We are talking about FPGA. Field-programmable Gate Array (FPGA) is an integrated circuit which can be configured by the user which provides it a major advantage over MCUs which have a fixed configuration. This enables the user to implement any digital circuit (YES! EVEN A PROCESSOR) on FPGA. The FPGA configuration is generally specified using a hardware description language like Verilog.
